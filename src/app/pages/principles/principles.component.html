Our Core Principles

The values that guide our community

At Librevore Village, we are guided by a set of core principles that form the foundation of our community. These principles inform our decision-making, shape our daily interactions, and create the framework for how we live together.

Voluntaryism

We believe that all human interactions should be voluntary, free from coercion, threats, or violence. Our community operates on the principle that individuals have the right to make their own choices as long as they don't infringe upon the rights of others.

In Practice:

  • Community decisions are made through consensus or voluntary agreement rather than majority rule
  • Members are free to participate in community activities as they choose
  • Dispute resolution is based on peaceful negotiation and mediation
  • Individual boundaries and property rights are respected
  • All contributions to community projects are voluntary

Healthy Living

We prioritize the health and wellbeing of our community members through natural, sustainable practices that nourish both body and mind. We believe in living in harmony with nature and making choices that promote vibrant health.

In Practice:

  • Growing organic, nutrient-dense food in our community gardens
  • Using natural building materials and methods that create healthy living spaces
  • Encouraging physical activity through outdoor work and recreation
  • Minimizing exposure to environmental toxins and pollutants
  • Sharing knowledge about nutrition, herbal medicine, and holistic health practices

Family Focused

We recognize families as the cornerstone of our community. Our village is designed to support the needs of families and provide an enriching environment for children to grow, learn, and develop naturally.

In Practice:

  • Creating safe spaces for children to play, explore, and connect with nature
  • Supporting homeschooling and alternative education approaches
  • Fostering intergenerational relationships and knowledge sharing
  • Designing housing and community spaces with families in mind
  • Encouraging collaborative childcare and parenting support

Self-Sustainability

We strive to meet our basic needs through local, sustainable means, reducing dependence on external systems and increasing our resilience as a community. Self-sustainability allows us to live more freely and in alignment with our values.

In Practice:

  • Growing a significant portion of our own food
  • Harvesting and managing water resources responsibly
  • Utilizing renewable energy sources when possible
  • Developing practical skills within the community for building, repairs, and maintenance
  • Creating local economic opportunities and exchanges

Community Cooperation

While we value individual freedom and autonomy, we also recognize the strength and benefits that come from voluntary cooperation. Working together allows us to accomplish more than we could alone, enriching all our lives.

In Practice:

  • Sharing tools, equipment, and resources
  • Organizing work parties for larger projects
  • Creating community spaces for gatherings and celebrations
  • Trading skills and services within the community
  • Supporting each other through challenges and hardships
